Alarm & Detection Systems

For the industrial or hazardous location client, Seitsa provides combustible, toxic, and noxious gas detection systems as well as explosion prevention and suppression systems. Many of the control systems offered are networkable and can be monitored at a central location in an operator or security command center. Additionally, the end user has the option to include voice evacuation and mass notification to inform the public in case of emergency (e.g., fire, tornado, etc.) or simply for general announcements.

Carbon Dioxide (CO2) Fire Suppression

Seitsa specializes in the design, installation, and maintenance of all types of Carbon Dioxide (CO2) fire extinguishing systems. CO2 is a cost effective solution that suppresses fires in a fast and efficient manor for a variety of applications. CO2 is a low cost inert gas that is electrically non-conductive and does not cause damage to property or equipment. The systems can be operated automatically or manually and adapted to protect single or multiple hazards simultaneously.

Clean Agent / Halon Alternatives

Clean agents are designed to extinguish all types of ordinary liquid, combustible, and electrical fires, from deep-seated fires in early stages to fully developed fires in later stages. Another main benefit to clean agent fire suppression systems is that they will not damage any adjacent equipment that is otherwise undamaged by smoke or fire. Lastly, the concentration of clean agent fire suppression systems is low enough that it will not cause any adverse effects to personnel caught in the fire hazard during discharge.

F.A.Q

Frequently Asked Questions

What types of fire protection systems do you install?

We offer a variety of systems, including fire alarms, sprinkler systems, fire extinguishers, smoke detectors, and emergency lighting, all tailored to your property's needs.

How often should fire protection systems be inspected?

Fire protection systems should be inspected at least once a year, though some components, like fire alarms and sprinklers, may require more frequent testing or maintenance depending on local regulations.

Are your fire protection systems compliant with local safety codes?

Yes, all of our systems are fully compliant with local and national fire safety codes and regulations to ensure the highest level of safety and performance.

How do I know if I need an upgrade to my fire system?

Our expert technicians can perform a thorough fire safety assessment to determine if your current system meets your needs or if an upgrade is necessary for enhanced protection.

What should I do if my fire alarm goes off?

If your fire alarm goes off, immediately follow evacuation procedures, ensuring everyone exits the building safely. Afterward, contact emergency services to confirm whether there’s an actual fire or a system malfunction.

How long does it take to install a fire protection system?

Installation time varies depending on the size and complexity of the system. Typically, residential systems can be installed within a day, while larger commercial or industrial systems may take several days to complete.
